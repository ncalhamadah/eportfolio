Research Methods and Professional Practice

The Research Methods and Professional Practice module is an in-depth study of research methodologies and professional standards in the computing field, preparing students to undertake rigorous research in practical settings. It focuses on the application of the scientific method, critical analysis of research strategies, and the development of research competencies. Emphasis is placed on understanding legal, social, ethical, and professional issues related to computing projects, alongside relevant codes of practice. The module aims to enhance students' abilities to conduct independent research, backed by continuous assessment, case studies, and project tasks, fostering the creation of a detailed academic portfolio.

Active-active architecture is a system configuration in which multiple, geographically dispersed instances of a system simultaneously run and process data. Each instance in this configuration is fully functional and capable of taking over if the other fails. This enables uninterrupted service in the event of a system outage in one location, as the instance in the other location continues to handle requests without interruption. Active-passive architecture is a system design strategy in which one system (the active) handles operations while a duplicate system (the passive) waits until the active fails.The passive system, usually distributed across multiple locations, mirrors the active system and is ready to take over operations at any time. If the active system fails, the passive system takes over, ensuring service. Disaster recovery and business continuity plans use this architecture for redundancy and resilience. Depending on how often the two systems sync, the failover from active to passive may take time.
‍
Cold standby systems are backups that are powered off or disconnected. This system only activates when the primary system fails or is down for maintenance.Disaster recovery is cost-effective with cold standby systems, which are usually located elsewhere. They allow system recovery but require significant downtime.The data on a cold standby system is usually backed up from the primary system at scheduled intervals, so data loss may occur between backups and system failure. Cold standby systems have higher RPO and RTO values than active-active or active-passive systems.
